• Encircled mountain

    Brief about him: This archaeological area is located within a mountain range surrounding the Zarqa River and its tributaries, 42 km to the southwest of the city of Mafraq and 1 km south of the village of Al-Qunia. The mountain rises above sea level (670 m) and surrounds the site by several important mountains such as Jabal Al-Mughniyeh, Al-Magnona Mountains, Al-Mukhadah Mountains and Hamid Mountains, as well as a large group of water springs that flow into the Zarqa River, such as Oyoun Khurasan, Oyoun Al-Deir, Ein Al-Qunia and Oyoun Al-Karm. At the end of the Copper Stone Age, the site developed to be a walled city at the top of the mountain with an estimated area of ​​thirty acres, and it was an important city At the end of this era and in the first phase of the Early Bronze Age. This site was famous for the presence of burials called stone monuments (dolmens) and stony tombs that spread in the Early Bronze Age.
