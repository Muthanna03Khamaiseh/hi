• Um aljamal

    The roots of settlement in the town of Umm al-Jamal go back to the Nabataean period, from the first century BC until the Mamluk Islamic period. Archaeological permits revealed the existence of a group of ancient dams designed to collect water or transform it into reservoirs distributed in and around the ancient city. Nabataean writings were revealed on some stones and a large altar with Nabataean inscriptions on it. A large number of shrines were engraved with the names of their owners.

  • Burqa Palace

    The roots of settlement in it go back to the Neolithic era, and it took a military dimension in the Roman era and a religious dimension in the Byzantine era. As for the Arab-Islamic period (Umayyad), this palace was built for recreation and hunting and away from the noise of cities. The palace has a bathroom, a mosque, and houses. It was used as a stop on the pilgrimage trail until the eighth century. In the palace there is a mosque dating back to the Umayyad era.

  • Alfadayn

    One of the important archaeological sites in Mafraq Governorate, built by the Greeks, and it was an important city in the Byzantine era and in the Islamic era, as it was on the pilgrimage route.
