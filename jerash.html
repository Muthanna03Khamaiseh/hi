• Ancient city

    Jerash is considered one of the most important archaeological cities in the world. The history of this city dates back to the time of its founding during the reign of Alexander the Great in the fourth century BC. This city was blessed with calm, stability and peace and was greatly influenced by the Roman civilization and became one of the ten cities (the Decapolis). Where the old city is still preserved its features of streets, columns, theaters, churches, temples, bathrooms and walls dating back to different eras.

  • Dibeen Reserve

    Dibeen Nature Reserve is one of the nature reserves in Jordan, located in the Jerash Governorate. It extends over an area of 8.5 square kilometers of mountainous areas. Founded in 2004, Dibeen is considered one of the driest areas in the world where Aleppo pine grows, with an average rainfall of approximately 440 mm per year.

    It is part of the ancient city where these public baths were common in the Roman period.
